THE HISTORY OF BODONI TYPEFACE

CHARACTERISTIC DEFINITION PAGE GUIDE
Spur Small extensions of letter strokes which dont quality as serifs PG. 1
Eye The closed counter of a lowercase e PG. 1
Finial A tapered portion of the end of the stroke of a curved letter such as "C" or "E" PG. 1
Ascender The upward stroke above the x height in a lowercase letter PG. 2
Decender The portion of a letter that extends below the baseline of a font PG. 2
Neck The stroke that connects the upper bowl and lower loop of a double-story G PG. 3
Ear A decorative flourish usually placed on the upper right side of the bowl on a lowercase G PG. 3
Loop Enclosed or partially enclosed counter below the baseline that is connected to the bowl by a link in a double-story G PG. 3
Bilateral serif A serif extending to both sides of the main stroke that is reflexive PG. 4
Tittle Small distinguishing mark such as the diacritic on a lowecase "j" or i"" PG. 4
Counter The area of a letter that is entirely or partially enclosed by a letterform or symbol PG. 5
Cross stroke A horizontal stroke that intersects the stem of a lowercase "F" or "T" PG. 5
Aperture Used to describe posture, the angle that bisects the upper and lower stroke is the aixs PG. 5
